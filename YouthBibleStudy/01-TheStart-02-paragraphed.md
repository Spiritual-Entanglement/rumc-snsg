# The Guy Who Didn’t Know How to Pray

## Let me start with a few questions.

1. Have you ever tried really hard to NOT do something you know you shouldn't, but you did it anyway?

2. Have you ever wondered if you really are not a good Christian?

3. If something tragic happened in your life… would you know how to really lean into your faith?

### I want to tell you a true story about a guy who faced those very questions.

This guy grew up in church. Good church. Good people.  In 9th grade, he decided for himself to be a follower of Christ.

He was very serious about giving his best to God, and envisioned that no matter what work he did as an adult, he would always be a part of his church and dedicated to Jesus Christ.

Yet…  almost immediately following Jesus got hard. After about two weeks, he found he still struggled with the same temptations. In fact, they seemed to get worse. Especially with girls, it was like they instantly got prettier.

He kept promising God that he would do better, and try harder, but he really had no clue how.

So this guy started asking people, “How do you actually grow spiritually?” Most of the answers sounded like this:
“Try harder.”
“If you were truly born-again, God gives you the power to resist every temptation."
"Pray more."
"Okay, How? I try but it isn't helping."
"Read the Bible more."
"How? I try that too.  Which parts?  Just anywhere?"

So he tried these things. But just random prayer and scripture was not cutting it. He read books about the Christian life. Lots of them. The books said the same thing: behave better, try harder. Pray more. Read the Bible more. But no explanation of HOW. Inside this guy kept thinking: Is that really all the Christian life is? Just trying harder? Is it all just willpower?  If so, I'm weak.  Years go by, and this guy is still trying and failing regularly. His "faith" really isn't changing him....not on the inside.

He starts a career. He marries and becomes a father. All this time he stayed in his church. He served. He tried to live a good life. But he is always trying to live the Christian life on his own strength.

### Then something really hard happened.

This guys marriage fell apart. The next Sunday he sat in church, alone, before the service began and had a couple painful thoughts: "Nothing about Christianity is helping me right now." "Am I just playing games here?  Is it a waste of time?" He went back a few more Sundays, but eventually, he walked away from the church.

He started exploring Eastern spirituality and philosophy. He had always been interested in that. But none of it helped him live a better life either. Of course now, he did not have to resist his temptations, and soon he just gave in to almost everything.

### More Years go by.

Slowly and reluctantly, this guy started drifting back toward church. Not because he believed again — mostly because of people in his life.

He got remarried. Because of his wife, he started to go with her to the same church he had left. His years away from the church and reading in Eastern religion, philosophy, new age stuff, even the occult, resulted in a massively deconstructed view of God and Christianity. He was basically Agnostic at this point.

But one day he wanted to resolve the question of God. Being back at church caused Him to ask questions again, so he prayed, “God… if you’re really there… prove it.”  Little by little, small things started happening in his life and around the church, and he could not help but thing God's hand was in it all. So, this guy decides he does believe in God again. He renews his dedication to Jesus Christ all over again.

And guess what? He was right back where he left off: willpower Christianity

### But a moment comes that changes everything.
His father is now in the hospital, and is dying. There is nothing more to be done; his organs are shutting down one after another. It's Sunday. Early in the day, only a few people can be in his room at a time. (This was just post-covid.) The hospital waiting room is quiet but filling up with family.

He is sitting in the waiting room, and realizes: God is there. This isn't something he has felt before. It feels like God is literally sitting in a chair next to him. God isn't fixing anything. God isn't explaining anything. Just… sitting there with him… like any friend would do.

### The shocking part:
all this time as a Christian, and he doesn't really know how to pray. The day this guy most needs to talk to God, and he doesn't know what to say or how to say it. It becomes obvious to him that he doesn't have to pray right now. It is almost like God is saying, don't worry about it; WE are going to fix that later.

Through the day, different people are coming by and praying with the family. Various chaplains. A nurse. These prayers are simple, but amazing. Every time, he feels God’s presence more strongly. And before this guy's father died, he made a decision. He would never get caught without prayer again. God said he would teach him how to pray, so this time he would be paying attention.

### So He Entered What He Called The School of Prayer
When this guy got home, after his father died, he dug out every old prayer book he had. He started figuring out how to use them. He tried several different methods of prayer. It started to have an effect on him, but it did take some time to find a way of prayer that really resonated with him. But he kept learning and making adjustments. Slowly things began changing inside him. His faith stopped being about trying harder. That's like an external force of will. No, eventually, faith started to flow from within. It started becoming about being connected to God.

### Then a Game Changer
At some point this guy found an older Christian — someone who had walked with God for many years. And that person helped him refine his prayers and scripture reading. He also showed him how to look for God in every part of his day. In other words, he finally found someone to help guide him in the Christian life.

His original instincts had been correct: go ask another Christian for help. Especially, in the recent evangelical protestant traditions, Christianity is viewed as an individual thing. Jesus is my "PERSONAL" savior. Discipleship and Faith Formation are nearly non-existent in Christianity today. It really is no ones fault, because in the late 1800s and most of the 1900s, most people went to church. The churches did not feel like they need to do discipleship because most people were in church already.

BUT, we are not supposed to figure out the Christian life by ourselves. Even the disciples didn’t. They followed Jesus around for 3 years. They asked questions. They learned from someone who had walked the path before them. In the early church, they devoted themselves to prayer, worship (communion), the teaching of the Apostles.

Over time, this guy learned, via his own study and the help of his mentor, the meaning of 1 Thessalonians 5:17, "Pray without ceasing".

Of course, he isn't perfect, but temptations, struggles of life, and even the good days are much lighter and easier to deal with once he learned how to grow in relationship with God. Now it is much easier to connect with God, even when is messes up. It like God is teaching him everyday how to get just a little better than the day before.

### His Story Speaks to One of the Most Important Things We Can do:
Look to our Christian community for guidance. We need to identify mature Christians we can trust, and ask questions. (Almost all mature Christians will know to keep conversations to themselves.) Ask them questions. Ask them how they pray. Ask them how they deal with temptation and doubt. Ask them how they stay close to God.

Our faith isn’t just about intellectually believing something. It’s about learning how to walk with God. Meaning how to actually have a relationship with Him. A relationship that makes a real difference in your life. Sometimes the best way to learn that…is from someone who has already been walking that road for a long time.

Most of his life this guy tried hard to live a Christian life. He kept failing because he was trying to do it with is own power. Once he learned how to stay close to God, he was able to realized the victory Jesus won on the Cross and His Resurrection; Faith began to flow from within.

- God's grace has always been at work in his life.
  - It found this guy while he was lost in this world
  - It forgave him his sins while he was still lost
  - and it refused to leave him that way.
