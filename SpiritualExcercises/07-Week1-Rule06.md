**The Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius of Loyola: Week 1, Rule 6**
The text of Rule 6:
> Sixth Rule (Spiritual Desolation: A Time for Initiative). The sixth: although in
desolation we should not change our first proposals, it is very advantageous to change
ourselves intensely against the desolation itself, as by insisting more upon prayer,
meditation, upon much examination, and upon extending ourselves in some suitable
way of doing penance. (SE 319)

The Sixth rule describes the second step in dealing with spiritual desolation.
* This is the most active and encouraging rule.
  * In desolation: **ACT**, take initiative! Resist darkness with faith, prayer, and scripture.
* Remember:
  * Consolation is **not** spiritual *winning*.
  * Desolation is **not** spiritual *losing*.
* Again, he reminds us to not change anything about our spiritual life or practices.
  * Not spiritual direction
  * Not any discernments made during the last time in spiritual *consolation*.
  * Not prayer commitments
  * Wait until clarity and consolation return.
* Rule 6 compliments rule 5.
  * **Don't** change your decisions or practices; **DO** change yourself.
  * We don't change direction or practice, but we increase effort.
* Active Interior Resistance:
  * **DO** change your resolve to fight the desolation itself.
    * Decide to actively resist the desolation.
    * Pay attention to your inner state: moods and feelings.
      * See them as tools the enemy could use to increase feelings of desolation.
        * Identify the moods and thoughts that pull you down: name them!
        * Name them in prayer before God.
        * Awareness is the first step in resistance.
      * Use prayer, inner self-examination, and scripture to resist giving into desolation.
    * Act against the desolation
      * If tempted no forego prayers <==> pray more.
      * If tempted to wallow in discouragement <==> actively remember how God has shown you grace and mercy in the past.
* We cannot bring ourselves out of desolation.
  * Only God can bring us out of desolation.
    * We depend on Him
  * There are times when God allows desolation.  Coming in Rule 9.
* The idea of Penance:
  * Embrace acts of penance as a resistance to desolation.
  * Remember that in our weakness God's grace works most powerfully.
  * Confession of sin is an act of penance.
  * Penance does not have to be harsh:
    * Penance is ***not*** about punishment.
    * Penance is about **freedom** not **guilt**.
    * the goal is to turn the heart toward humility and dependence on God
* The goal is spiritual freedom
  * The ability to follow God no matter how we feel: consolation or desolation.
    * Freedom to do as we decide, **not** to do as our feelings and mood dictate.
      * or the blowing winds of temptation.
* Rules 7 and 8 will address attitudes that sustain our efforts: trust and patience.
