**The Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius of Loyola: Week 1, Rule 5**
The text of Rule 5:
> Fifth Rule (Spiritual Desolation: A Time for Fidelity). The fifth: in time of desolation never make a change, but be firm and constant in the proposals and determination in which one was the day preceding such desolation, or in the determination in which one was in the preceding consolation. Because, as in consolation the good spirit guides and counsels us more, so in desolation the bad spirit, with whose counsels we cannot find the way to a right decision. (SE 318)

The Fifth rule describes the first step in dealing with spiritual desolation.
* **This rule is one of the most important**.
  * It is also one of the hardest to follow...
  * This is **the first defense** against panic and confusion.
  * **DO NOT** alter your prayer life/habit/routine when you find yourself in desolation.
* Remember:
  * Consolation is **not** spiritual *winning*.
  * Desolation is **not** spiritual *losing*.
* In desolation, the evil spirit will do everything in its power to discourage prayer and reading of scripture.
  * **BUT Remember** God allows times of desolation so we can grow in perseverance and a strengthening of our faith, hope, trust, and love.
  * You will feel as if prayer is ineffective.
  * You will feel unworthy to pray or even look at scripture.
 s * Many discouraging thoughts or emotions will be applied to keep you from prayer.
  * **YET** our prayers are even **more** meritorious at this time.
    * This is the time when the rubber meets the road.
    * We are praying out of faith, hope, trust, and love instead of just chasing a spiritual high.
* **REMEMBER** God has not changed; our ability to perceive Him has changed.
  * God is still as close to you as always even if you cannot *feel* it.
  * Fidelity relies on **GOD's** constancy, **not** our emotional state.
* Do not change anything from the last time you felt close to God, your last time in consolation
  * Not spiritual direction
  * Not any discernments made at that time.
  * Not prayer commitments
  * Not spiritual direction.
  * Wait until clarity and consolation return.
* Prayer and scripture are your lifeline to God.
* If the evil spirit can get you to stop reading scripture and praying, you will start to grow weaker spiritually.
  * The 'habit' of prayer is broken it is harder to get restarted sometimes.
    * Keeping our heart ready for God's grace makes it easier to receive when consolation returns.
  * In a spiritually weakened state, you are much more susceptible to temptations.
* Now is the time to build fidelity and fortitude when faced with struggle.
  * We build spiritual muscle by praying especially when we do not feel like it.
  * Keeping your prayer and scripture reading during desolation helps to build up our resistance, patience, and resilience during any struggle.
* This is why it is so important to build good prayer and scripture habits in consolation, so we can maintain them during desolation.
  * During desolation, prayer and scripture, may seem almost mechanical and dry.
  * The value of perseverance and patience will become evident, and a cause for praise of God, when the next state of consolation comes.
  * You will 'feel' spiritually stronger, and **yet** humble too, because **GOD** gets you through that time, not your own efforts.
    * Yes, **your** effort made you show up,
    * **BUT GOD** gives the increase and the victory.

**Stay Faithful**
Decisions made or changed in darkness do not lead to God's light.  Keep faithful to resolutions made in consolation.

**Next**
Rules 6-8 are going to show us *how* to act in desolation.  Not by changing decisions or habits, but by changing ourselves via prayer and patience.
